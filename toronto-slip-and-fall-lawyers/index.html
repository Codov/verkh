Why you should work with Toronto slip and fall lawyers

Toronto slip and fall lawyers know that contrary to what some victims think, slip and falls can be complicated and tricky to self-represent, in other words, to go to court without a personal injury lawyer.

For example, did you realize the law to file notice of a claim has been tightened to 60 days after a slip and fall due to ice or snow accumulation? And notwithstanding what type of case you have, it will be contingent on being able to prove damages were caused by negligence of the occupier, a.k.a. the owner, business/residential tenant, or whoever has physical possession of the property or premises. 

By the way, how do you prove negligence in the first place? Thats just for starters. Worse, so-called self-represented litigants are held to the same legal standard by judges as lawyers. That can be dauntingand actually work against your best interests.

Our Toronto slip and fall lawyers have experience in handling these types of personal injury claims. Theyll walk you through the process that in Ontario establishes culpability under the Occupiers Liability Act. The Act contains the written laws that provide for when all property owners can be held financially responsible for injuries sustained in a slip and fall accident on their premises.

The law imposes on owners whats called a reasonable duty of care. That means theyre obliged to ensure that visitors will be reasonably safe when welcomed to their homes, businesses, or public places. They must ensure the property is clear of dangers, hazards, or disrepair. But heres the deal: the duty of care doesnt attach liability as a matter of course; thats where proof comes in.

Toronto slip and fall lawyers talk about negligence, proof, and duty of care

Negligence When Toronto slip and fall lawyers take on your case, a primary focus will be on proving liability, which means establishing under the Ontario Negligence Act (2004) who is responsible and in what proportion. The occupier has a duty to keep their property safe. So, if they knew or shouldve known about a hazard yet didnt fix it, they could be found negligent. The law would say the breach must have caused the harm, and the resultant damages wouldnt have happened in the first place.

Proof Our Toronto slip and fall lawyers have ways to prove your case, starting with your doctor, who could solidify your claim that the injuries you sustained were a result of a slip and fall accident. Witness statements on your behalf would ward off predictable defence claims the injury happened due to your carelessness. And if there are security videos of the accident or photographs from the area where you fell showing the defect that caused the accident, they would be strong evidence.

Duty of Care The term duty of care pops up a lot in a discussion of slip and falls. Thats because its at the root of it all. Occupiers must ensure the safety of their visitors at all times to a reasonable level. But whats reasonable? Thats the complicated part. Owners have to take steps to eliminate a risk, and if thats not possible, then there must be a process in place to safeguard visitors from harm.
